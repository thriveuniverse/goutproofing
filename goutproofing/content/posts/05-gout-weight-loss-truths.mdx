So you have just been told you have gout – pretty depressing


Then they add that you have to give everything up – even more depressing 


The final nail in the coffin of doom is the weight. If most of us are honest with ourselves we know that that there may be a few extra pounds hanging around, but it always felt like something to worry about tomorrow. Now the doctors are hammering it home as if your whole existence depends on this (and it kind of does).
I don’t like being the barer of bad news, but I also believe in honesty.

**You have to lose weight!**

Don’t panic though because the reason you have to do this is so you can do the other things which are so much more important in life, like sharing a glass of wine with your family, being a part of your work teams social life, participating in family gatherings without panicking or just being able to give a toast without a sense of doom.
Why is weight important? 


•	**Higher uric acid production**: Fat tissue (especially visceral fat) is metabolically active — it over-produces uric acid and makes the kidneys excrete less.

•	**Insulin resistance: Excess weight** → insulin doesn’t work properly → kidneys hold onto more uric acid → blood levels rise.

•	**Chronic low-grade inflammation**: Fat cells release inflammatory cytokines → makes the immune system more trigger-happy with crystals.

•	**Joint stress**: Extra weight = more mechanical load on joints → easier for crystals to cause trouble.

Every 5–10 kg you lose drops serum uric acid by roughly 1 mg/dL — _permanently_. 

Many men who lose 15–25 kg never need medication again.

This isn’t about becoming a gym bro or never eating pizza again.

It’s about getting light enough that a glass of wine, a birthday cake, or a Christmas dinner doesn’t feel like playing Russian roulette with your toe.

_Lose the weight not to punish yourself — lose it to get your life back._
