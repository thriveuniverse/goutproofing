Living with gout doesn’t mean starving yourself or giving up flavour.

My approach is simple: **stay satisfied, keep things practical, and focus on what lowers uric acid without making life miserable**. No extremes — just hacks from my own routine that have helped keep flares at bay.

These come from personal trial and error (plus research). I’m not a doctor — consult yours. But if you’re overwhelmed by the usual *“give up everything”* advice, this might help you get started.

---

## Morning Routine: Start Strong, Stay Hydrated

Mornings set the tone. I prioritise hydration and a hearty breakfast to avoid hunger crashes — which spike stress and uric acid.

- **Water first:** Chug a pint (≈500ml) of water right after waking. Wait ~40 minutes before food.
- **Optional blood test:** Every few days, I check uric acid with my home meter (not daily — no obsession). It helps spot patterns without paranoia or feeling like a pin cushion.
- **Coffee:** Black or with a splash of low-fat milk. Coffee has been linked to lower uric acid risk for many — and honestly, don’t skip it if it’s your joy.

### Breakfast
- **3-egg cheese omelette**  
  Eggs are low-purine, protein-packed, and filling. Add spinach or tomatoes for an extra anti-inflammatory boost.

**Result:** No hunger, steady energy — and uric acid stays stable.

## Lunch: Weird but Wonderful Swaps

My wife handles lunches (we both work online), and her creations grew on me fast. The focus is **low-purine, veggie-heavy, satisfying dips and spreads**.

We eat together — which makes it social, not a chore.

- **Tahini dip:**  
  Blended sesame seeds with fresh lemon juice, yoghurt, and garlic (skip garlic on meeting days).  
  Tahini is moderate-purine but nutrient-dense; yoghurt supports digestion and may help lower uric acid.  
  Dip with carrot sticks.

- **Soft cheese on cucumber:**  
  Strong blue cheese (e.g. Roquefort) on thin cucumber slices. Sounds odd. Tastes incredible.  
  Cucumber is ultra-low purine; cheese adds protein without spiking levels.

- **Endive leaves with Boursin:**  
  Black pepper or shallot flavour works best. Bitter-fresh crunch meets creamy comfort. Zero cooking.

- **Mixed veggie stir-fry:**  
  Courgettes, onions, shallots, tomatoes, bell peppers, chilli, and beans in olive oil.  
  Serve warm with melted cheese or plain.  
  Onion/garlic family foods are anti-inflammatory and may help uric acid excretion.

**Outcome:** Light but filling lunches — no afternoon slump, no flare triggers.

## Snacks: If You Need Them

I rarely snack (big meals help), but when I do, it’s seeds.

We buy in bulk:
- **Pumpkin seeds:** Magnesium-rich, supports kidney function (key for uric acid clearance).
- **Sunflower seeds:** Vitamin E for skin health and antioxidants to fight inflammation.

Low-purine, crunchy satisfaction. Lightly roasted for flavour (and a touch of chili if you want a kick).

---

## Dinner: Hearty but Early

I moved dinner earlier — eating late raises overnight uric acid. Meals are still substantial, just smarter.

- **Veggie risotto:**  
  Onion base, peas or beans, cream for luxury.  
  Use a brown / wild / red rice mix for a lower glycaemic hit.

- **Chicken cauliflower cheese:**  
  Comfort food without guilt. Chicken is moderate-purine; cauliflower is low.

- **Stir-fry leftovers:**  
  Yesterday’s veg and meat, curried or plain, over a *small* portion of rice.  
  Rice is a side, not the star — avoids insulin spikes that trap uric acid.

**Rule of thumb:** Veggies first, protein second, carbs minimal.  
Cook with olive oil — anti-inflammatory bonus.

## Benefit Foods: My Daily MVPs

These aren’t magic bullets — but they’ve helped my levels and overall health.

- **Onions, garlic, shallots**  
  Anti-inflammatory all-stars. May boost uric acid excretion and reduce flares.  
  Add them everywhere — flavour without calories.

- **Yoghurt**  
  Probiotics support gut health, which is linked to better uric acid control.  
  Stick to plain, low-fat.

- **Pumpkin seeds**  
  Magnesium and zinc support kidney function and inflammation control.

- **Sunflower seeds**  
  Vitamin E and antioxidants help fight oxidative stress common in gout.

### Drinks
- **Zero Tonic**  
Water is your best buddy - you need to knock back a fair amount of this. I aim for a pint an hour (measured in pints because a pint glass on the desk is easier than smaller ones or having to refill from a bottle, it just seems to work). My most important discovery for drinks was Zero Tonic Water. This is my evening go-to, add lemon and and ice and its refreshing, tastes okay and isn't just more water!!

**Bonus:** Still hydrating but defines the evening, and lets me feel like a functioning adult not a patient.

Track what works **for you**. My flares are down, energy is up, and life feels normal-ish again — which is the real goal.
